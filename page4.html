Additional Credential Options

To renew OR reinstate your credential, you must submit proper documents verifying that you successfully meet one (1) of the following options.

Renew or Reinstate Credenital 

To renew your credential OR reinstate one that has already expired, you must submit proper documents verifying that you successfully meet one (1) of the following three options. These options may not be combined for renewal and may not apply to every credential type. 

Option 1: Professional Development 

Submit official certificates of completion and/or official transcripts verifying satisfactory completion of the equivalent of 120 clock hours or 8 college credit hours of professional development (PD) training activities completed within the previous four years. See PD activity domain table below.

  1. Applicants must submit proper documentation verifying satisfactory completion of the equivalent of 120 hours of professional development (PD) training activities completed within the four year term of the credential being renewed.
  2. The equivalent of 120 hours equals 120 actual clock hours of instruction/activity time completed; or 8 college credit hours; or a combination of both. One college credit hour equals 15 clock hours.
  3. A minimum of the equivalent 60 hours must be directly related to the content area of the credential being renewed. The additional hours may align with any of the areas as indicated in the “acceptable PD activity domain table” shown below.
  4. The hours may be accrued by completion of college coursework or completion of training activities delivered by the employing local education agency, national education organizations, related industry organizations, and/or accredited PD provider organizations

Option 2: IMPACT Performance rating

If you are employed with the DC Public Schools (DCPS) agency and have an assignment in the area of the credential being renewed, submit all pages of your end-of-year final IMPACT performance rating reports for each applicable school year. You must have achieved an effective or higher rating for at least three of the previous four years.

Option 3: Passing Scores on Subject Content Exam 

In lieu, of having documents verifying completion of Option 1 or Option 2, you may present passing scores for the subject content exam indicated below for re-issuance of the credential. However, exams must be/have been passed within 12 months of the date the credential application is submitted.

Add Endorsement to Pre-existing Credential

Qualified applicants may be issued a standard four-year renewable
teaching credential in an additional teaching subject areas(s), if the applicant:


(1) Holds a valid standard teaching credential in another subject area that is not due to expire within the next 60 days

(2) Has passing scores for the required Praxis II subject content exam in the teaching area of the endorsement being sought; OR  

for teaching subject areas where a subject content exam has not been adopted the applicant holds a completed degree with a major in the applicable area.

  1. An FBI personal criminal history report (PCHR) issued within the previous twelve months. For information on how to obtain your PCHR report and procedures for current DC Public Schools (DCPS) employees, please read the attached instructions.
  2. All pages of examinee score report verifying passing scores for the subject content and pedagogy exams required for the endorsement area.
  3. Official transcripts from the institution where a degree with a major in the area of the endorsement has been completed. Applicants who completed studies at institutions outside of the United States must submit a proper foreign credential evaluation report regarding those studies.
  4. Applicants, who hold a valid full teaching credential in another state and have passed comparable exams in the other state, may submit a copy of their license and test score report for a review by OSSE to determine if those scores may be applied in lieu of the required Praxis exams.
